\part{The Problem of Topology}
\label{part:topology}

\epigraph{The difficult part is no longer generating isolated insights. The difficult part
is imposing recoverable topology on a massive graph of partially overlapping trajectories.}{}

\vspace{1cm}
\noindent Most of the conceptual work already exists as a distributed semantic field with
diffuse boundaries. A monograph functions partly as a constraint geometry over that field:
it establishes admissible traversal paths through the corpus so readers can progressively
stabilize the conceptual dependencies rather than encountering them as isolated fragments.

Part~\ref{part:topology} establishes why a unified process ontology requires a new traversal
architecture rather than a new set of isolated claims, introduces the primitive vocabulary on
which the remainder of the monograph is built, and argues that the form of the text is a
consequence of its content.
