## 3. Entropy as Accessibility: The Geometry of Your Future

Traditional physics often defines entropy loosely as “disorder.” Within the RSVP framework, entropy is instead interpreted geometrically:

S = log Vol(Γ)

Entropy is the logarithmic volume of admissible future trajectories.

It measures the amount of “room to move” available to a system.

## The Geometry of Choice

### 1. High Accessibility (High S)

The system possesses many possible continuations. This corresponds to organized indeterminacy—a state of high creative openness where multiple propagative resolutions remain admissible.

### 2. Low Accessibility (Low S)

The geometry of futures contracts. As the system becomes constrained to a narrow trajectory bundle, predictability increases. This is Harmonic Lock—the emergence of stable classical persistence.

### 3. Impulsive Reset

Occasionally, a system undergoes a sudden accessibility expansion resembling a Dirac-like impulse. Existing stability collapses and the geometry of possible futures briefly expands before reconverging into a new coherent regime.

When S becomes both low and temporally stable, the system enters deadband compression. No meaningful information needs to propagate because the future is already strongly determined by the present configuration.

Silence therefore corresponds not to inactivity, but to maximal predictability.
